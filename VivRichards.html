***About Sir Viv Richards.***

Sir Vivian "Viv" Richards is a former West Indian cricketer who is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. He was born on March 7, 1952, in Antigua. Richards was known for his aggressive and dominant style of batting, and he played a crucial role in the success of the West Indies cricket team during the 1970s and 1980s. Some key highlights of Viv Richards' cricketing career include: Dominant Batting: Richards was known for his powerful and stylish batting. He was especially famous for his ability to take on fast bowlers with fearless and aggressive stroke play. Captaincy: He served as the captain of the West Indies cricket team from 1984 to 1991. World Cup Success: Richards played a significant role in the West Indies' World Cup victories in 1975 and 1979. In the 1979 World Cup final, he scored a match-winning century. Records: He held several records, including the highest individual Test score by a West Indian batsman at the time (which has since been surpassed), and he was the first cricketer to score more than 100 international centuries. Cricketing Legacy: Viv Richards is not only remembered for his on-field exploits but also for the impact he had on the game. He is considered a cricketing legend and a symbol of West Indian cricket's dominance during its peak. After retiring from international cricket, Viv Richards has been involved in various coaching and commentary roles. His contributions to cricket have earned him a place in the International Cricket Council's Hall of Fame, and he remains an iconic figure in the cricketing world.
